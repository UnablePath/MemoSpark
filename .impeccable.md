# Impeccable — MemoSpark

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary segment:** Ghana university students who want an **all-in-one** product for academic life, wellbeing, and connection—not a scattered stack of generic tools.

**Typical context:** On phone between classes, late-night study blocks, and quick check-ins during the week. Interfaces must feel fast, legible, and emotionally aligned with pressure and momentum (not cold enterprise software).

### Brand Personality

**Three words:** **seamless**, **interesting**, **enticing**.

- **Seamless:** Flow between timetable, mood, streaks, Stu, and social without cognitive overload; interactions feel continuous, not fragmented.
- **Interesting:** Distinctive visuals and motion that reward attention—never bland “template SaaS.”
- **Enticing:** The product should feel worth opening again—warm invitation, not guilt or anxiety as the default emotional driver.

### Aesthetic Direction

**North star:** Form follows function, while the **heart of the brand** reads as **unique, modern, and overt**—confidence without shouting, personality without clutter.

**References (what to borrow, not copy):**

1. [Rhythm Dialogues](https://rhythmdialogues.ca/?ref=siteinspire) — **bold design elements** and clarity of **use cases** / narrative structure.
2. [Parallel Society (Logos)](https://ps.logos.co/?ref=siteinspire) — **unusual typographic and layout choices**, event/culture energy, and a sense of **culture + convergence** without default corporate grids.

**Theme:** **Dark by default**, with a **cohesive path to light mode** (adaptive / user-switchable light that still feels like MemoSpark, not a separate product).

**Color:** **Avoid purple** as a brand or default UI accent—primary and accent families should stay in other hues (e.g., warm editorial neutrals + jade/green lineage already in tokens, or another intentional non-purple palette).

**Anti-references:** *Not specified yet*—add explicit “never look like X” examples as the brand hardens (e.g., generic purple-gradient SaaS, clinical hospital UI, or toy-like overload).

### Design Principles

1. **Overt but legible:** Strong layout and type choices are encouraged; information hierarchy and scanability stay non-negotiable for stressed students.
2. **Seamless cross-surface:** Patterns (spacing, nav, Stu, gamification) should feel like one system, not unrelated screens stitched together.
3. **Dark-first, light-capable:** Default dark experience is the hero; light mode is a full sibling theme, not a washed-afterthought.
4. **No purple-by-default:** Treat purple as out of bounds for primary brand and default chrome unless a future explicit brand decision overrides this document.
5. **Accessibility:** Meet **WCAG 2.1 AA** as the baseline for shipped UI (contrast, focus, semantics). No additional special requirements were specified beyond standard inclusive defaults; respect **prefers-reduced-motion** for motion-heavy treatments.
